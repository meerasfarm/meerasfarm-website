A World

There is a place, forty-five minutes from the hum of Navi Mumbai, where time moves differently. Where the brick on the walls remembers the hands that formed it in Godhra, Gujarat. Where a Balinese statue stands quietly beside a Nepali prayer bell.

This is Meera's Farm — not a retreat built from a brief, but a life collected slowly, over decades of travel and quiet wonder, made generous enough to share.

From the Host

A Letter
from Ravi

Dear guest, welcome to a place that was never designed to be a destination — it simply became one. Meera's Farm grew out of a life spent travelling, collecting, wondering at how much beauty the world holds, and wanting to bring it home.

The bricks came from Gujarat. The furniture from Saharanpur. The statues from Bali, the textiles from the Americas. Each piece has a story, and I hope you'll ask about them.

My passions are thoughtful spaces, meaningful conversations, and genuine human experiences. Everything you see here is an expression of that. I hope Meera's Farm gives you the pause you were looking for.

"Not built from a brief. Built from a life."

With warmth,

Ravi

Superhost · Mahalungi, Maharashtra

The Land —
Mahalungi

Forty-five minutes from Navi Mumbai, the city dissolves into farmland, and farmland into sky. Mahalungi is a village that city-dwellers have largely forgotten — which is precisely what makes it so precious.

From the farm, you can see mountains in one direction and the city skyline in another. Two worlds, held in the same gaze. It is a contrast that never grows ordinary.
